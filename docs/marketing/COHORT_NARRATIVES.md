# BookBounty Cohort Narratives: The Student vs. The Retiree

To build a product that resonates, we must understand the high-stakes life transitions that drive a user to scan their first book. For BookBounty, these stories center on two primary cohorts: **The College Student Purge** and **The Retiree Downsize**.

---

## 1. The College Student Purge
**The Core Driver:** Speed, Cash-on-Hand, and Mobility.

### The Persona: "Alex"
- **Age:** 22
- **Context:** Graduation is in three days. The lease ends at noon on Saturday. Alex is moving across the country for an entry-level job and only has room for what fits in a sedan.
- **The Problem:** A four-year accumulation of heavy textbooks, impulse-buy novels, and "essential" philosophy reads. Alex needs them gone, but Alex also needs gas money for the drive to Seattle.
- **The Friction:** Traditional buy-back programs are a rip-off. Local used bookstores are picky and slow. Alex doesn't have time to list 50 books on eBay individually.

### The BookBounty Journey
1. **The Intent:** Alex sets the **"Financial Optimizer"** goal. The priority is liquidity.
2. **The Triage:** Alex scans a stack of textbooks. The AI instantly flags a 2023 Biology text: **SELL ($85)**. It flags a 2018 Econ text: **DISCARD** (Outdated).
3. **The Result:** In 15 minutes, Alex has a "Sell" pile worth $240 and a "Donate" box for the rest.
4. **The "Aha" Moment:** BookBounty generates a bulk-listing export. Alex sells the high-value books to a specialist site and drops the rest at a donation bin on the way out of town.
5. **The Value Proposition:** BookBounty turned "heavy clutter" into "gas money" in under an hour.

---

## 2. The Retiree Downsize
**The Core Driver:** Emotional Legacy, Space-Making, and "Result-Driven" Clarity.

### The Persona: "Martha"
- **Age:** 68
- **Context:** Martha is moving from a four-bedroom family home to a two-bedroom condo. She has a library of 1,200 books collected over forty years.
- **The Problem:** Every book feels like a memory. Martha is paralyzed by decision fatigue. She wants her books to "go somewhere they matter," but she doesn't know which are valuable and which are common.
- **The Friction:** Martha is overwhelmed by the sheer volume. She fears throwing away something "important" or "rare" but doesn't have the energy for exhaustive research.

### The BookBounty Journey
1. **The Intent:** Martha sets the **"Legacy Preservation"** goal. The priority is meaningful re-homing.
2. **The Triage:** Martha scans an obscure volume on local 19th-century architecture. The AI flags it as **Offer for Acquisition** (V3 Vision). A regional university library is looking for this specific title.
3. **The Result:** Martha feels a surge of pride. Her book isn't just "stuff"; it's a contribution to an archive. For her collection of thrillers, the AI suggests **DONATE** to the local VA hospital, providing clear reasoning: "High-demand leisure reading for residents."
4. **The "Aha" Moment:** The "Shelf Impact" dashboard shows Martha that her 1,200 books have been reduced to 200 "Keep" titles that truly define her next chapter.
5. **The Value Proposition:** BookBounty transformed a "burdensome hoard" into a "curated legacy" and a series of "meaningful gifts."

---

## 3. Comparison of Value

| Feature | Student (Alex) | Retiree (Martha) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Goal** | Liquidity (Cash) | Legacy (Meaning) |
| **Metric for Success** | Dollars per Minute | Space Gained / Impact Made |
| **AI Tone Preference** | Direct, Market-Focused | Empathetic, Contextual |
| **V3 Interaction** | "How much is the procurement fee?" | "Who will be reading my book?" |

By speaking both languages, BookBounty captures the two largest "exit points" of the physical book market.
